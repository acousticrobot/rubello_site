REFLECTED COLOR.

A common concern in Rubello's three-dimensional paintings and sculptures: by placing a colored surface very near and at a right angle to a white surface, a subtle, colored shadow is cast upon the latter.

Illuminated Column, 1973, acrylic on wood

"It glows," Rubello says about this device. "But I call it a fugitive thing because you either see it or you don't. It all has to do with the painting's relationship to light."
