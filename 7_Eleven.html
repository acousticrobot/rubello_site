7-ELEVEN.

In April 2014, the first 7-Eleven store to operate in Detroit in eleven years opens on Jefferson Ave., east of downtown. In anticipation of its opening and the subsequent opening of a second store downtown, large banner ads featuring photo collages of different animals wearing Slurpee mustache straws appear on several downtown buildings, including one, featuring a cat, on the north exterior wall of the Julian C. Madison Building.

Photo by Paul Beshouri for Curbed Detroit.

Before the ad's installation, David Rubello's mural Color Cubes, painted on the north exterior wall of the Madison Building in 1973, is painted over. The ad remains on the wall for just a few months. Its removal reveals a four story, grey-brown rectangle where Color Cubes used to be.
